nothingness — icon variants

10 candidates in the v6_monk+ aesthetic: pure black field, white forms, monospace, light weights, 1px hairlines.
Each tile shows the icon at preview size, plus a 56px (app drawer) and 32px (status bar) version to test legibility at small sizes.

01 corners
NOTH — first four letters of "nothing", positioned at the corners of an unseen square. Reads as a word at large size and as a structured logotype at small size.
N · O · T · H
02 corners
Same composition, lowercase, hair-thin (200 weight). Echoes the v6_monk hero ("nothingness|") which itself is light-weight lowercase.
n · o · t · h
03 corners
VOID — a sister concept to "nothingness". Already used in the codebase (void_screen.dart, void_browser.dart). Short, four characters, perfect for the corner pattern.
V · O · I · D
04 corners
NESS — the last four letters of "nothingNESS". Less literal than NOTH; reads as a cryptic monogram. Distinctive at small size because of the repeated S.
N · E · S · S
05 typographic
Pure quote of the v6_monk hero ("nothingness|" reduced to first letter + caret). Strongest brand fidelity to the actual UI.
n|
06 glyph
∅ — the empty set / null glyph. Already used in v6_monk's empty-search row ("⌀ nothing"). Direct semantic match for "nothingness". Reads at any size.
∅ empty set
07 glyph
A single hairline circle. The most reduced form: nothing enclosed by an outline. The "0" of zen and of zero. Dangerously generic but maximally on-brand.
○
08 structural
The 1px progress hairline at the bottom of the viewport, lifted into the icon. The most extreme reduction: the icon is literally just the play-progress bar. Probably illegible at 32px.
— hairline
09 structural
"…/n" — the auto-truncated breadcrumb from v6_monk, frozen into a logo. Tail-of-path metaphor: "you are here, at the end of a long tree, in nothingness". File-tree-native.
…/n
10 grid
3×3 dot grid with the centre absent — "nothing in the middle". Reads as a tic-tac-toe with a missing piece. Suggests structure-around-emptiness, which is exactly what the player is.
